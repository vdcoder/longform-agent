Chapter 1 introduces the book's central argument: that software team reliability
is a system property, not an individual or process property. It defines
reliability as comprising three components — predictability, resilience, and
sustainability — and explains how they reinforce each other. The chapter
describes the common failure pattern (short-term pressure crowding out
long-term investment) and frames the manager's role as designing a system that
resists that pattern. The remaining chapters address technical debt and on-call
culture as the two primary domains where reliability is built or lost.
