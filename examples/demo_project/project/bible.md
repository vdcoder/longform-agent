# Project Bible — Building Reliable Software Teams

## Title
Building Reliable Software Teams

## Audience
Engineering managers, senior engineers, and team leads who want to build
sustainable, high-performing teams in product organisations.

## Tone
Practical and direct. Evidence-informed but not academic. Avoid buzzwords.
Favour concrete examples over abstract frameworks.

## Scope
This book covers team composition, engineering process, technical debt
management, on-call culture, and organisational communication. It does not
cover sales, fundraising, or executive leadership.

## Chapter Overview
1. Introduction — why software teams fail and a framework for thinking about reliability
2. Technical Debt — what it is, how to measure it, how to reduce it without stalling product work
3. On-Call Culture — designing a sustainable rotation and blameless incident review

## Style Notes
- Use second person ("you", "your team") for direct advice sections.
- Use first-person plural ("we") when discussing shared engineering practice.
- Keep paragraphs short (3–5 sentences).
- Prefer numbered lists for sequential steps; bullet lists for non-sequential items.
- Every chapter should end with a short "Key Takeaways" section.
